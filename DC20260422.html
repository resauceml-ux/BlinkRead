The seventh book teaches that nothing in life is truly new, and that what we call wickedness is something we have already seen many times before. Everywhere, in stories, in cities, and in daily life, the same patterns repeat. Because of this, we should not be surprised or disturbed when unpleasant things happen. They are part of the natural order and are temporary. Everything that exists is familiar and short lived, and understanding this helps calm the mind. Our peace depends on keeping our thoughts clear and aligned with reason. It is always within our power to interpret events correctly. When something happens, we can choose to see it truthfully instead of reacting emotionally. External things do not control us unless we allow them to affect our judgment. By focusing only on what belongs to our understanding, we remain steady and undisturbed. Life can feel repetitive and full of distractions, like public shows, conflicts, and meaningless struggles. Many people chase these things, but they are no different from animals fighting over scraps. A wise person stands firm among these distractions, calm and free from anger. The value of a person is not determined by what they chase, but by the quality of their thoughts and actions. We must examine everything carefully, both words and actions, understanding their true meaning and purpose. Reason should guide us, and we should use our abilities fully when they are sufficient. If not, we should not hesitate to seek help from others for the sake of the common good. Seeking praise is pointless, because those who once received admiration are now forgotten, along with those who praised them. We should not fear the future. Whatever happens, we will be able to face it with the same reason we use now. Everything in the universe is connected and follows a natural order. There is one shared reason and one shared truth among all rational beings. Recognizing this unity helps us accept events as they come. All things change constantly. Matter dissolves and reforms, and even memory fades quickly. For a rational being, acting according to nature and reason is the same. We are part of a larger whole, like members of a body working together. True kindness comes from understanding this, not from obligation, but from knowing that helping others is also helping ourselves. External events cannot harm us unless we judge them as harmful. We must remain good regardless of what others do or say, just as gold remains gold no matter how it is treated. Our mind has the power to remain calm and free from fear unless we allow false opinions to disturb it. The body may feel pain, but the mind does not have to suffer with it. Change is natural and necessary. Nothing can exist without change, and even death is simply another transformation. Many great people have lived and died, and their existence has passed into the flow of time. We too will be forgotten, just as we forget others. This realization should not cause fear, but instead encourage us to focus on living rightly now. When others do wrong, we should understand that they act based on what they believe is good. This makes it easier to respond with patience instead of anger. If we once believed the same things, we should forgive them. If we no longer do, we should still show kindness to those who are mistaken. We should focus on the present moment and not imagine future troubles. Appreciate what we have now without becoming too attached to it. The mind can find peace within itself when it practices justice and self control. By removing false opinions and examining everything carefully, we can live simply and truthfully. Death and pain should not trouble us. Death is either a return to nature or a transformation, and pain is either brief or bearable. The mind can remain calm even when the body suffers. Praise and reputation are meaningless, as they quickly fade and depend on the opinions of others. A person of true greatness sees life as small and does not fear death. What matters is living with virtue, not preserving life at all costs. We should aim to act justly and live according to reason, regardless of circumstances. Looking at life from a broader perspective helps us detach from trivial concerns. Everything in history repeats itself. Whether one lives for a short time or a long time, they will see the same patterns. All things return to their source, and nothing remains permanent. Understanding this helps us accept life as it is. We should not compare ourselves to others based on physical abilities or status, but on character. True worth lies in kindness, modesty, and resilience. Whenever we act according to reason and for the common good, there is no reason for sorrow. We must focus on our own path and not be distracted by others. Our purpose is to serve the common good, control our desires, and avoid error. By doing this, we achieve fulfillment and happiness. Live each day as if it were your last, without excessive emotion or indifference. Be sincere and steady. Do not be quick to judge others, as even the gods show patience. Instead of trying to control others, focus on improving yourself. Doing good is its own reward. There is no need to seek recognition or repayment. Just as beneficial actions help others, they also benefit us. Continue doing what is right without expecting anything in return. Finally, remember that everything in the universe follows a rational order. Whether events are planned or simply part of a greater system, they contribute to the whole. Accepting this truth brings peace and allows us to live in harmony with the world.
