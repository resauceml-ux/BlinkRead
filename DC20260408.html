In ancient Babylon the city was known as the richest in the world but it was not always that way Its wealth was not built by luck but by the wisdom of its people They first had to learn how to become wealthy before the city could prosper After a great war King Sargon returned to Babylon and found a serious problem The great projects like irrigation canals and temples had been completed but now the people were struggling Laborers had no work merchants had no customers and farmers could not sell their goods The flow of gold had stopped and many people had nothing left The Royal Chancellor explained that the gold had not disappeared it had simply moved into the hands of a few rich men Most people had spent everything they earned and had nothing saved The King questioned why only a few were able to become wealthy The Chancellor answered simply because they knew how He explained that wealth is not unfairly given but earned by those who understand its principles It is not right to take from those who have learned how to build wealth but instead others should be taught the same knowledge The King then asked if it was possible for everyone to learn how to become wealthy The answer was yes but they needed someone who understood money and success They agreed that the best person to teach this knowledge was Arkad the richest man in Babylon Arkad was brought before the King and asked how he became wealthy He replied that he simply took advantage of opportunities that were available to everyone He started with nothing except a strong desire to become rich The King explained that Babylon could only become truly prosperous if many of its citizens became wealthy not just a few Therefore he asked Arkad to teach his knowledge to others so they could also learn how to build wealth Arkad agreed and offered to teach one hundred men the principles that changed his life Soon a group of one hundred men gathered in the Temple of Learning to hear Arkad speak He stood before them and explained that he was once poor just like them but through knowledge and discipline he was able to change his life He told them that his purse was once empty and that he hated its emptiness He wanted it to be full and growing so he searched for solutions to fix this problem Over time he discovered seven simple cures that helped him grow his wealth Arkad explained that these cures were not complicated but they required consistency and discipline He would teach one lesson each day so the students could fully understand and apply each principle He emphasized that before anyone could become wealthy they must first learn how to manage their own money Building wealth starts with small steps and simple habits that are repeated over time The first and most important step is learning how to fill your purse This means keeping a portion of everything you earn instead of spending it all Most people fail because they spend everything they make and never allow their money to grow Arkad wanted his students to understand that wealth does not come from earning more alone but from managing what you already have Even a small amount saved regularly can grow into something significant over time He encouraged them to listen carefully discuss the lessons and apply them in their own lives because knowledge alone is not enough it must be used The purpose of these lessons was not just to make a few people rich but to create a society where many people understood wealth and could build a better future for themselves and their families Arkad reminded them that anyone could start regardless of their situation because he himself started with nothing but determination He believed that once a person learns the right principles they can change their financial future The seven cures for a lean purse were designed to guide people step by step from having nothing to building lasting wealth Each cure builds on the previous one creating a strong foundation for financial success This was the beginning of a transformation not just for the students but for the entire city because when more people understand how to manage money wealth begins to grow everywhere The lesson is simple wealth is not reserved for a few it is available to anyone willing to learn and apply the right habits It begins with awareness discipline and the decision to change how you handle money And just like Arkad taught his students the first step is always the same learn how to keep a part of what you earn and let it grow over time
