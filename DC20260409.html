In ancient Babylon Arkad the richest man in the city was tasked with teaching others how to build wealth He gathered a group of men from different professions and began by showing them a simple truth everyone earns money through their work no matter how small or large their income Each person already has a stream of income flowing into their purse The problem is not earning it is what they do with what they earn The first cure Arkad taught was to start fattening the purse He explained this through a simple analogy if a man puts ten coins into his purse and spends only nine over time his purse will grow The principle is clear save at least one tenth of everything you earn This is the foundation of wealth Arkad emphasized that even though this seems simple most people fail to follow it He himself began this way and once he started saving consistently his financial condition began to improve Surprisingly he found that living on nine tenths of his income did not reduce his quality of life Instead his ability to manage money improved and opportunities seemed to come more easily The second cure is to control expenditures Arkad explained that people often confuse necessary expenses with desires No matter how much a person earns their expenses will naturally rise to match their income unless they actively control them This means that without discipline even high earners can remain poor He advised his students to create a plan identifying which expenses are truly necessary and which are simply desires By prioritizing needs and meaningful wants and cutting unnecessary spending a person can live comfortably while still saving A plan is not meant to restrict life but to protect and grow wealth The third cure is to make gold multiply Saving money is only the beginning wealth grows when money is put to work Arkad explained that money should be invested so that it generates more money He shared his own experience of lending money to a shield maker who used it in his business and paid back both the loan and a return By reinvesting both the original money and the earnings Arkad was able to grow his wealth steadily This is the principle of compounding money earning more money over time He stressed that true wealth is not just the amount of money a person holds but the income that continues to flow into their life The fourth cure is to guard wealth from loss Arkad warned that many people lose their savings because they chase risky opportunities or trust the wrong people He shared a personal mistake where he entrusted his savings to a brick maker who claimed he could buy valuable jewels only to be deceived From this Arkad learned that investments should only be made with people who are knowledgeable and experienced in that field He emphasized the importance of protecting the original money before seeking high returns It is better to earn a small safe return than to risk losing everything The fifth cure is to make of thy dwelling a profitable investment Arkad explained that owning a home can be a powerful step toward financial stability Instead of paying rent to a landlord a man can invest in his own home and gradually pay it off Over time this reduces living expenses and builds long term value Owning a home also brings pride stability and comfort to a family making life more fulfilling while also contributing to financial growth The sixth cure is to ensure a future income Arkad taught that every man should prepare for the future especially for old age and for the protection of his family This can be done through consistent saving and long term investments Even small amounts saved regularly can grow into large sums over time providing security and peace of mind He emphasized that planning for the future is essential because a time will come when a person can no longer work as they once did Without preparation this can lead to hardship but with foresight it can lead to comfort and independence The seventh cure is to increase the ability to earn Arkad explained that a persons earning power is one of their greatest assets By improving skills gaining knowledge and becoming more effective in their work a person can increase their income He encouraged continuous learning and self improvement as a way to stay valuable and competitive Those who strive to improve themselves will naturally find more opportunities and higher rewards Success begins with a strong desire and a clear goal and grows through discipline persistence and action Arkad concluded by reminding his students that wealth is not reserved for a select few but is available to anyone willing to learn and apply these principles The seven cures are simple but powerful when practiced consistently They transform not only a persons finances but also their mindset habits and future By saving controlling spending investing wisely protecting wealth planning ahead and improving oneself any person can build lasting prosperity and live a life of abundance
