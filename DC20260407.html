In ancient Babylon there lived a man named Arkad who was known as the richest man in the city He was generous with his family and gave freely to charity yet his wealth continued to grow faster than his spending His old friends came to him confused because they had once been equals They studied together played together and lived similar lives yet Arkad had become wealthy while they struggled to survive They asked why fate had favored him and not them Arkad explained that it was not luck or fate but knowledge and discipline He said that those who fail to build wealth either do not understand the laws of money or do not follow them He warned that luck is unreliable because sudden wealth often leads to waste fear or misery True wealth comes from understanding and applying certain principles When Arkad was young he realized that wealth allowed people to enjoy life more fully It provided comfort freedom and opportunity He decided that he did not want to stand on the outside watching others enjoy life He wanted to take part in it Despite being the son of a poor merchant with no inheritance and no special talent he committed himself to learning how wealth is created He found work as a scribe but despite working long hours he had nothing to show for it because all his earnings were spent on daily needs One day a wealthy money lender named Algamish offered him a small reward to complete a task Arkad used the opportunity to ask how he could become rich Algamish agreed to teach him if he finished the work After completing the task Arkad received his first lesson A part of all you earn is yours to keep At first Arkad did not understand because he thought everything he earned already belonged to him Algamish explained that most of what people earn is spent on others food clothing and services so they are actually paying everyone except themselves He taught Arkad to save at least one tenth of everything he earned and to treat that portion as his own Arkad began doing this consistently and noticed that his life did not become harder even though he was saving money He simply adjusted his spending Later Arkad made a mistake by investing his savings with a brickmaker who claimed he could buy valuable jewels This turned out to be a bad decision because the brickmaker lacked knowledge in that area and Arkad lost his savings Algamish corrected him by teaching another lesson Seek advice only from those who are experienced and knowledgeable in the field Arkad learned from this mistake and started again He continued saving one tenth of his income and eventually invested it wisely with someone skilled in business His savings began to earn returns and he received regular income from his investments However he made another mistake by spending the profits instead of reinvesting them Algamish explained that by doing this Arkad was destroying the growth of his wealth because he was consuming the future potential of his money Arkad then learned the importance of making money work for him Every coin saved should become a worker that generates more income and those earnings should also produce more income Over time this creates a powerful cycle of growth As Arkad continued applying these principles his wealth increased steadily He developed three key habits living below his means seeking wise advice and making his money grow Through discipline and patience he became successful and eventually managed the estate of Algamish After the old man died Arkad inherited a share of his wealth When Arkad shared his story his friends realized that his success was not due to luck but to consistent action and clear principles He emphasized that opportunity comes to those who are prepared and disciplined He compared success to a fisherman who studies his craft over time and becomes skilled rather than relying on luck He also explained that willpower alone is not enough Instead success comes from setting clear tasks and completing them consistently Even small actions done daily build confidence and discipline Arkad addressed the concern that if everyone became wealthy there would not be enough wealth to go around He explained that wealth is not fixed but grows when people create value When money is spent it does not disappear but flows to others who continue to use it and build more wealth Finally Arkad gave practical advice to his friends First always save a portion of your income at least one tenth Second control your expenses so you can live within your means Third make your savings grow by investing wisely Fourth protect your wealth from loss by avoiding risky decisions Fifth plan for the future so you and your family are secure Sixth seek guidance from experienced people Seventh enjoy life responsibly without overspending Some of his friends ignored the advice while others took it seriously Those who applied his teachings gradually improved their financial situation They began saving investing and growing their wealth safely Over time they transformed their lives The key lesson is that wealth is built through simple consistent habits Anyone can achieve it by saving regularly investing wisely and making money work for them It is not about luck talent or background but about understanding and applying timeless principles
