At the head of a caravan traveling across the desert rode Sharru Nada a wealthy and respected merchant of Babylon Though he appeared confident and successful he was troubled not by danger or hardship but by the young man beside him named Hadan Gula the grandson of his former partner Arad Gula Sharru Nada felt a deep sense of gratitude toward Arad Gula and wanted to help his grandson but found it difficult because Hadan was proud wasteful and believed that work was only for slaves Hadan dreamed of living a life of luxury wearing jewels and spending money freely without effort which worried Sharru Nada greatly As they rode Hadan questioned why Sharru Nada continued to work so hard despite his wealth He said that if he had such riches he would spend his life enjoying pleasure and avoiding labor Sharru Nada did not argue directly but instead decided to teach him through a story from his own past He revealed that he himself had once been a slave a fact that shocked Hadan Sharru Nada explained that in his youth he had been sold into slavery because of his brothers wrongdoing and his familys inability to pay a debt He was chained with other men including Megiddo a hardworking farmer Zabado a lazy thief and a man called Pirate who was angry and rebellious As they were marched toward Babylon Megiddo spoke about the value of work saying that work was his best friend and had given him everything he once owned Zabado however mocked work and believed in avoiding effort whenever possible Pirate relied on anger and violence Sharru Nada listened to all of them but was unsure what to believe When they reached Babylon Sharru Nada saw the terrible conditions of slaves working on the city walls Men were beaten exhausted and often died from the harsh labor He became afraid and realized that his future depended on being chosen by a good master At the slave market he followed advice to present himself as willing and eager to work This attitude helped him stand out and he was purchased by a baker named Nana naid Under his new master Sharru Nada worked diligently He learned baking and quickly became skilled Wanting more than survival he thought of a way to earn money for himself He proposed making extra honey cakes and selling them in the streets His master agreed to share a portion of the earnings This was the turning point in his life Through effort and initiative Sharru Nada began earning small amounts of money which he saved with the hope of buying his freedom Each day he sold cakes and slowly built a small income He gained confidence and pride in his work and began to see that work was not a burden but an opportunity He also met Arad Gula who was then a slave merchant Arad Gula noticed his dedication and encouraged him saying that such effort could lead to success These words gave Sharru Nada even more motivation to continue As time passed Sharru Nada improved his situation He gained regular customers and increased his earnings He also met Megiddo again who was now doing well because of his hard work Megiddo had earned trust and responsibility and was on the path to regaining his freedom Meanwhile Zabado who avoided work suffered greatly under harsh conditions and Pirate who resisted authority was eventually killed These examples showed Sharru Nada the consequences of different attitudes toward work Eventually Sharru Nada faced another setback when his master fell into debt due to gambling Because Sharru Nada had been used as collateral he was taken by a new master and forced into extremely difficult labor digging a canal The conditions were brutal and he began to lose hope During this time he reflected on the different approaches of his former companions He realized that avoiding work like Zabado or resisting like Pirate led only to suffering while embracing work like Megiddo led to progress He chose to continue working with determination even in hardship Soon after he was unexpectedly rescued by Arad Gula who had been inspired by Sharru Nadas words and work ethic Arad Gula had gained his own freedom and success and sought out Sharru Nada to repay him He bought Sharru Nadas freedom and made him his business partner In that moment Sharru Nada realized that his willingness to work had changed his destiny and brought him opportunity Returning to the present Sharru Nada explained to Hadan that work had been the key to his success and also to Arad Gulas success It was not luck or inheritance but effort discipline and persistence that created wealth He emphasized that work builds character earns respect and opens doors to opportunity Hadan listened carefully and began to understand He realized that his grandfathers success was not based on comfort or ease but on hard work and determination He saw that his own attitude had been misguided and that his desire for luxury without effort would only lead to failure Inspired by the story he decided to change He removed his jewelry and resolved to follow the path of hard work starting from humble beginnings As they entered the gates of Babylon Hadan rode behind Sharru Nada with respect no longer seeing work as something beneath him but as the true path to success and fulfillment The lesson was clear that work when embraced with willingness and purpose becomes a powerful force that can transform a mans life and lead him from hardship to prosperity making him truly fortunate
